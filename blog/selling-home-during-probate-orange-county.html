I've handled probate sales for a long time now. The first thing I tell every executor is the same thing. The court sets the pace, not you.

Selling a Home During Probate in Orange County

When somebody dies, the family has a major life change on their hands. The house is usually the biggest asset in the estate. Selling a home during probate in Orange County means the court is involved, and that changes the timeline, the paperwork, and sometimes the price.

The Court Process Takes Time

Probate doesn't happen instantly. The estate has to go through the court system, and that can take months before you're cleared to list.

I tell my clients to be patient and be consistent. The families who plan around the court's schedule have a much easier time than the ones who fight it.

Pricing a Probate Property Is Different

A probate sale often needs a probate referee's appraisal, and the sale price has to hold up against it. That's not the same as picking a number off a website estimate.

I've been through a lot of cycles down here. I look at what's actually selling on this stretch of coast, not what an algorithm guesses.

What Executors Should Do First

Get the paperwork in order before you think about the sign in the yard. Letters of administration, the will if there is one, and a clear picture of who the heirs are.

Then call someone who has done this before. Probate has its own rules, and an agent who doesn't know them can cost the estate real money.
