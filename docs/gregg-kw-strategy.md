# **Keyword Explanations**

Below is a plain-language explanation of each keyword: why it was chosen, what kind of person is searching it, and where it fits on the site.

## **Tier 1: Primary Targets**

These five terms are the ones we build the site around. They are specific enough that you can actually rank for them, and they match exactly what you do. Tier 1 terms go on your highest-traffic pages and anchor all your on-page SEO work.

**1\. real estate agent San Clemente CA:** 

The core local term. When someone in or relocating to San Clemente searches for an agent, this is the phrase they use. The competition here is mostly directory aggregators (Zillow, Yelp, Coldwell Banker) rather than individual agent sites with deep content. That gives you an opening. A well-structured homepage and consistent use of this phrase across the site builds authority over time.

**2\. probate real estate agent Orange County:** 

This is your single strongest differentiator. There is real search volume here, and the agents currently ranking for it are niche specialists, which is exactly your positioning after 40 years focused on estate transitions. The people searching this phrase are either executors trying to understand the process or family members who already know they need a specialist. High intent, right audience.

**3\. selling home during divorce Orange County:** 

Divorce-related home sales are emotionally loaded and legally complex. People searching this are actively in a transaction and need someone they can trust to stay neutral and move the deal forward. Multiple agents are creating content around this query but no one has locked it down. A strong Specialties page and an FAQ section targeting the common questions here (who signs what, how proceeds are split, what happens if one party refuses) gives you a real shot.

**4\. probate real estate California:** 

A broader version of keyword 2 that captures people in the early research phase, before they've decided on an agent or even a county. The search volume is higher, but so is the competition. The best use of this term is on a dedicated guide page that explains the probate process in plain language, positions you as the expert, and funnels readers toward a consultation. It also performs well in AI-generated search answers, which is increasingly where this type of informational query lands.

**5\. estate sale real estate agent San Clemente:** 

Lower competition than the broader probate term, but very high intent. Someone searching this exact phrase has a property to sell and is looking for an agent right now. Very few individual agent pages target this specific combination, which makes it a practical win for a site with a modest domain authority.

## **Tier 2: Supporting Terms**

These five terms support the Tier 1 keywords by building out community pages and content that answers the questions people ask before they pick up the phone. They drive traffic at the research stage and warm up leads before they reach out.

**6\. real estate agent Dana Point:** 

Dana Point is the second most important city in your service area and carries its own search volume. Community and market pages optimized for this term also get picked up by the Google Local Pack, which shows maps and agent profiles at the top of results. Pairing this with a Dana Point community page on the site reinforces local authority.

**7\. San Juan Capistrano homes for sale:** 

An active search term with buyers at the top of the funnel. A community page for San Juan Capistrano on realestateandloans.com targets this term and can link to more detailed information on greggmcelwee.com. It also signals to Google that the site covers the full South Orange County service area, not just San Clemente.

**8\. selling inherited property California:** 

High search volume in the research phase. People searching this are trying to understand their options, often for the first time, after losing a parent or relative. A well-written guide page that answers the key questions (do I have to go through probate, what are the tax implications, how long does it take) builds trust and positions you as the go-to expert before they ever call anyone.

**9\. how long does probate take in California:** 

This question consistently appears in the People Also Ask box on Google for nearly every probate-related search. A blog post or FAQ entry that answers it clearly has a real shot at a featured snippet, which puts your site at the very top of results. The honest answer (9 to 18 months typically, depending on estate complexity and court backlog) is also a natural lead-in for why working with an experienced agent matters.

**10\. divorce real estate agent Orange County:** 

A sibling to keyword 3 but targeting the agent selection query rather than the process question. Someone searching this already knows they need an agent; they are deciding who to call. This term belongs on the Specialties page alongside the divorce-focused content, using slightly different language than keyword 3 so the two support each other rather than compete.

## **Tier 3: Long-Tail Pipeline**

These five terms feed the blog and guide content over time. Lower competition, more specific intent. Each one is a natural blog post or guide page topic that builds the site's authority and brings in readers who are very close to making a decision.

**11\. buying a home in San Clemente:** 

A lower-competition entry point for buyer-side content. Gregg works with buyers as well as sellers, and a practical guide to buying in San Clemente (what neighborhoods look like, what the market is doing, what first-time buyers need to know) attracts a different audience than the probate and divorce content, broadening the site's reach without diluting the core positioning.

**12\. South Orange County real estate market 2026:** 

Market update content that gets refreshed annually. Competitors are doing this consistently and it generates steady search traffic because buyers and sellers are always looking for current market data. A well-written market update in plain language, published on a regular cadence, also signals to Google that the site is actively maintained.

**13\. sell house during probate Orange County:** 

A more specific variant of keyword 2 that targets someone who already has a probate property and needs to sell it, rather than someone who is hiring an agent on behalf of an estate. Slightly different intent, worth a dedicated guide page or FAQ entry that addresses the court confirmation process, the role of a probate referee, and how to navigate the sale without delays.

**14\. court-ordered home sale California:** 

A narrow but essentially uncontested search term. Very few individual agent pages address this directly, but it is a real scenario that comes up in both contested divorces and probate disputes. A guide page or FAQ entry targeting this phrase could rank with minimal competition and reaches someone at a critical decision point who needs exactly the kind of experienced, steady-handed agent Gregg is.

**15\. coastal Orange County realtor:** 

A branding and geographic authority term rather than a high-intent transactional query. Lower commercial intent on its own, but useful for reinforcing that Gregg's practice covers the full coastal South OC corridor, not just one city. Works well in the homepage and About page copy on greggmcelwee.com, and helps the two domains support each other when both appear in search results.

# **Suggested Next Steps**

Once you have reviewed this list and are comfortable with the direction, here is how we put it into action:

* Site audit: map each keyword to specific pages on realestateandloans.com and identify gaps where a page does not yet exist.

* FAQ section: draft a FAQ block for the Specialties page using the questions that surface in the People Also Ask boxes for keywords 3, 4, 9, and 14\.

* Guide pages: build out the hidden-but-indexable /guides/ directory with one page per Tier 2 and Tier 3 topic. These do not need to be in the main navigation but should be findable by Google.

* Blog seed posts: use keywords 11, 12, and 9 as the first three blog post topics. These are evergreen or annually renewable and give the autoblog pipeline a head start.

* Google Search Console: once the site is live, monitor which queries are generating impressions. The actual data will tell us which terms to double down on after 60 to 90 days.
