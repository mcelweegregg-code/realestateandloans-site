South Orange County, start to finish.

Six communities. One agent who knows them all.

Where I work

I have spent nearly four decades working the stretch of coastline and inland communities that make up South Orange County. I know these neighborhoods from the inside, not from a map. Whether you are buying, selling, or navigating a more complex situation, local knowledge is what makes the difference.

In-depth community guides, market data, and neighborhood lifestyle info live on greggmcelwee.com. The cards below link directly to those guides.

San Clemente

My home community and the heart of my practice. Known for its Spanish Colonial architecture, surf culture, and some of the most desirable coastal real estate in Orange County. I moved here four years ago to be closer to the water, and I have not looked back.

Explore San Clemente →

Dana Point

A harbor city with a relaxed coastal character and strong demand for both primary residences and investment properties. Home to Doheny State Beach and a thriving marina community.

Explore Dana Point →

San Juan Capistrano

One of California's most historic towns, with a distinctive small-town character and a real estate market that attracts buyers looking for something beyond the typical coastal tract home.

Explore San Juan Capistrano →

Mission Viejo

A master-planned community with well-maintained neighborhoods, strong schools, and consistent demand from families and move-up buyers. One of the most stable markets in South OC.

Explore Mission Viejo →

Laguna Hills

A quieter inland community with good value relative to its coastal neighbors and easy access to the 5 and 405 corridors. Popular with buyers who want South OC without the coastal price premium.

Explore Laguna Hills →

Laguna Niguel

An affluent hillside community with sweeping views, upscale neighborhoods, and a real estate market that holds its value through most cycles. Strong demand from luxury and move-up buyers.

Explore Laguna Niguel →
