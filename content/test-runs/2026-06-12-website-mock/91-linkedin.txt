Almost 40 years selling homes in South Orange County, and this year I finally admitted something. My website was costing me business.

I'd see new signs going up on streets I've worked for decades, names I didn't recognize. Meanwhile I was paying every month for a site that turned out to have almost no search value at all. At one point a service I paid for published a post on my blog about keeping snow off your porch. I live in San Clemente. No snow.

So I rebuilt. A new site with real content, written from my own experience instead of recycled filler. One thing this business has taught me: people can tell when something's authentic, and so can Google.

If you're an agent, take ten minutes this week and read your own website the way a stranger would. If it doesn't sound like you, that's the version of you prospects are meeting before you ever shake their hand.

And if you're thinking about a move anywhere on this coast, that's the business I'm actually in. Take a look at the new site, and if something there is useful to you, pass it along.
