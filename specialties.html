Specialty

Probate & Estate Transitions

When someone passes away owning real property in California, that property typically has to move through probate before it can be sold. The court oversees the process, sets timelines, and requires specific procedures that most standard real estate transactions never encounter. An executor or administrator is appointed to manage the estate, and they carry a fiduciary duty to handle the sale properly and get fair value for the heirs.

That is a lot of moving parts. Gregg has been working probate sales in Orange County for years and knows the process well. He understands the court confirmation requirements, the timeline constraints, and the documentation involved. More importantly, he knows how to keep the sale moving forward so the estate is not sitting on a property longer than it needs to be.

These transactions also carry emotional weight. The family is often grieving, sometimes disagreeing, and navigating a process none of them planned for. Gregg approaches these situations with the patience and steadiness the circumstances require, keeping the focus on a fair, clean outcome for everyone involved.

If you are an executor, administrator, or family member dealing with a probate property in South Orange County, Gregg is one of the more experienced agents you will find for this specific type of sale.

Divorce Real Estate

When a marriage ends and the couple owns a home, that property has to be dealt with, sold, bought out by one party, or transferred under a court order. It is rarely simple. There are two parties who may not be communicating well, attorneys on both sides, and a court that may be setting the timeline and terms.

The agent in these situations needs to be neutral. Not aligned with one side, not pushing an agenda, just focused on getting the property sold fairly and on time. Gregg has handled divorce sales and understands what the role requires. He works with both parties and their respective counsel, keeps communication factual and professional, and does not let the personal dynamics of the situation affect the outcome of the sale.

Court-ordered sales come with their own procedural requirements and deadlines. Gregg knows how these work in California and can navigate the process without creating additional friction for parties who already have enough of it.

If you or your attorney are looking for an experienced, neutral agent to handle a divorce property sale in Orange County, Gregg is worth a conversation.
